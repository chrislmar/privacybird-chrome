Privacy Bird for Chrome


Thanks for installing Privacy Bird!

This extension analyzes the privacy policies of the web sites that you visit. It will alert you if the privacy policy of the web site you are currently browsing violates your privacy preferences. Make sure you check the options page to set your privacy preferences and manage the alerts you will receive.

Evaluation results of the current web site's privacy policy will be indicated by the bird icon in the upper-right corner of your browser (near the wrench).

  • A green bird means that the policy matches your preferences.
  • A red bird means that the policy violates one or more of your preferences. The number on the icon indicates the number of actual violations.
  • A yellow bird means that the policy is either invalid ('X') or does not exist ('?').
  • A gray bird means that the extension has been disabled for the current site. This occurs when you are viewing a non-http page or when there is an error with the extension or any supporting resources (e.g. the remote validation server).
Notification sounds will also be played when a site's policy matches or violates your preferences. These notifications can be disabled via the options page, where you can enable additional sounds as well.

For more information about a given web site, click on the icon to reveal the popup window. If applicable, the popup will display a list of warnings regarding the ways in which the policy violates your preferences. It will also give you a summary of the site's entire privacy policy. If the site has a valid policy, there will be a link at the bottom of the popup window for a "Nutrition Label" style representation of the privacy policy (as developed by the CMU CUPS Lab).

Privacy Policy:

Privacy Bird for Chrome does not collect any personal information. The only data it uses are the URLs of the web sites that you browse. This data is maintained for the operation of the extension. It is stored locally within your browser and will be deleted whenever you uninstall the extension.

This extension does not uphold your browser's promises in Incognito Mode. If you enable Privacy Bird for Chrome in Incognito Mode, it will continue to record the URLs of the web sites you recently visited for performance reasons.
